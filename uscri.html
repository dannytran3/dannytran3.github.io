    As the web developer intern at USCRI, I was assigned the task of recreating the office intranet site. The site is used for employee-only forms, documents, files, and links to helpful resources. The office uses SharePoint Online 2013 as a part of Office 365 to host their intranet site. Most of my technical learning experience at USCRI was adapting websites to work with SharePoint. Using Master Pages and SharePoint Designer, I developed a solid workflow that suited my HTML/CSS background. Below are some of the many drafts I created over the time I spent at USCRI. All demos below are functioning without SharePoint and their code is not entirely representative of how it would be in SharePoint. The code is also un-minified for easier reading.

    Working under the IT Department, my supervisor and the director wanted to create a site with full page backgrounds. This initial site was eventually scrapped because of its functional inefficiency. We agreed that employees visit the site to grab a form or document and to help them get there quickly should hold a higher priority than the prominent photography used in this iteration. None the less, it was my first foray into parallax websites and I learned a lot.

    This was one of my favorite iterations. I pitched it after getting the initial concept functional. I had planned a sidebar filled with the home page's main links and a small carousel for announcements. The divided blue background would each have their own photo background to represent the link. This iteration was turned down due to a lack of a full width slideshow for announcements.

    The current iteration utilizes a material design philosophy. After asking employees what they mainly use the site for, I found that most use it to access their team site, which is their department's hub for even more specific documents. With 19 different team sites, keeping them in a side bar was the best way have it always accessible while navigating the site. At this stage the site is using web parts tied to SharePoint so no more functioning demos. Some web parts allow employees to post announcements and for the IT department to update the site more easily without editing the HTML/CSS. Pictures below.
